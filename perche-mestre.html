Why Mestre?

A historical journey through the turning points that shaped a city in search of its collective soul.

Historical Timeline

1917

Porto Marghera hub

Creation of the industrial heart that redefined the city's purpose.

1926

Annexation

Mestre is merged with Venice, losing its administrative autonomy.

1960

Expansion

Rapid suburban growth and the rise of car-centric motorization.

1972

Tangenziale

Opening of the ring road, reinforcing the "city in transit" model.

1985

Piazza Ferretto

Pedestrianization and restyling of the city's historical heart.

2004

San Giuliano

Inauguration of the massive public urban park on the lagoon.

2018

M9 Museum

A new cultural district aimed at urban regeneration.

2026

New Station

A future intermodal hub to redefine urban accessibility.

The Necessity for uSeed

Mestre’s history is defined by a succession of top-down interventions and planning driven by priorities external to its social fabric, which have fragmented the territory and hindered the formation of a shared identity.

"A legacy of discontinuity requires a tool for reconnection."

This legacy of discontinuity—where the city has repeatedly been reshaped by centralized, monofunctional choices—is precisely what creates the need for a platform like uSeed.

The necessity arises from having experienced decades of imposed transformations that left little room for spontaneous aggregation, leaving a void that now requires tools capable of reconnecting the territory, grounded in its complex historical reality.
