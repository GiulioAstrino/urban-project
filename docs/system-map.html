System Map Analysis & Impact Report

1. Key Stakeholders

Organizations

Businesses, cultural associations, and parishes prioritizing inclusion.

Businesses & Investors

SMEs, investment funds, urban developers, and retail.

Landowners

Private owners, real estate funds, and railway/industrial entities.

Residents

Families, students, commuters, and migrants.

Negative Dynamics & Bottlenecks

Bureaucratic Friction

Rules block the desire to reuse abandoned spaces, causing implementation delays and stagnation despite new ideas.

Passive Speculation

Owners choose "owner waiting" due to poor conditions, decreasing property value and increasing resident tolerance for decay.

Urban Decay Spiral

High vacancy rates lead to physical decay and social stigma, creating a self-feeding downward spiral.

Social Maintenance Loop

Decay reduces community care and social participation, ultimately resulting in even more physical decay.

Situational Intervention (Risk of Exclusion)

Localized improvements can raise property values and rents, disproportionately impacting students and migrants, leading to gentrification.

The Solution: uSeed Positive Loops

Local Social Activation (Transition Loop)

Facilitating reuse builds trust, leading to more participation and driving common action for further reuse.

uSeed Shared Values

Breaks Bureaucratic Friction through "Idea Collection" and "Collective Approval," encouraging targeted urban action by authorities.

uSeed Safety Perception

Interrupts the Decay Spiral and Social Maintenance Loop by encouraging local events and social interactions.

uSeed Networking

Connects public organizations and supports community action, creating job opportunities and promoting Youth Retention.

uSeed Trust

Builds faith in the platform and entities. Collective approval leads to better data collection and more effective, credible policies.
