Urban Development & uSeed Integration

Systemic Mapping Analysis & Impact Report

1. Key Stakeholders

Public Admin

Regional authorities, municipality, and local planning agencies.

Organizations

Businesses, cultural associations, and parishes prioritizing inclusion.

Investors

SMEs, investment funds, urban developers, and retail.

Landowners

Private owners, real estate funds, and industrial entities.

Residents

Families, students, commuters, and migrants.

Negative Dynamics

Bureaucratic Friction

Rules block the desire to reuse abandoned spaces, causing implementation delays and stagnation despite new ideas.

Passive Speculation

Owners choose "owner waiting" due to poor conditions, decreasing property value and increasing resident tolerance for decay.

Urban Decay Spiral

High vacancy rates lead to physical decay and social stigma, creating a self-feeding downward spiral.

Situational Intervention Risk

Localized improvements can raise property values, potentially excluding students and migrants via gentrification.

uSeed Positive Loops

Local Social Activation

Facilitating reuse builds trust, leading to more participation and driving common action for further reuse.

Shared Values

Breaks friction through "Idea Collection" and "Collective Approval," encouraging targeted action by authorities.

Safety Perception

Interrupts the Decay Spiral by encouraging local events and social interactions, reclaiming the streets.

Networking & Trust

Builds faith in institutions through better data collection and more effective, credible, community-backed policies.
