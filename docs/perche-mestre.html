Why Mestre

Historical Timeline of Mestre

1917–1970: Porto Marghera industrial hub creation
1926: Annexation of Mestre to Venice
1960–1990: Suburban expansion and motorization
1972: Opening of the Tangenziale A52
1985–1997: Pedestrianization and restyling of Piazza Ferretto
2004: San Giuliano Park inauguration
2018: M9 Museum opening
2026: New Mestre train station

The Necessity for Useed

Mestre’s history is defined by a succession of top-down interventions and planning driven by priorities external to its social fabric, which have fragmented the territory and hindered the formation of a shared, diffuse identity.

This legacy of discontinuity—where the city has repeatedly been reshaped by centralized, monofunctional choices—is precisely what creates the need for a platform like Useed. The necessity arises from having experienced decades of imposed transformations that left little room for spontaneous aggregation, leaving a void that now requires tools capable of reconnecting the territory, grounded in its complex historical reality.
